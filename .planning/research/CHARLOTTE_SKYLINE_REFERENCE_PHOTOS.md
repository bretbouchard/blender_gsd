## Executive Summary

Charlotte, NC (known as "Uptown") features a distinctive modern skyline with several buildings featuring programmable LED lighting systems. The most prominent is the **Duke Energy Center (550 South Tryon)** with its 45,000+ LED lights and hourly light shows. This research compiles reference photos and technical details for digital twin recreation.

---

## Primary Reference Buildings

### 1. Duke Energy Center / Wells Fargo Tower (550 South Tryon)

**Building Specifications:**
| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Address | 550 South Tryon Street, Charlotte, NC |
| Height | 786.5 ft (240m), 48 occupied floors (51 total) |
| Opened | January 2010 |
| Architect | Tvsdesign |
| Owner | Wells Fargo |
| Certification | LEED Platinum |

**LED Lighting System:**
- **45,000+ programmable LED lights** integrated into facade
- Hundreds of color-changing LED and metal halide luminaires
- **Hourly light shows** at the top of each hour after sunset
- Operates sundown to midnight, then ~1 hour before sunrise
- **Energy cost:** Less than $2/night to operate
- **Lighting designer:** Gabler-Youngston Architectural Lighting Design
- Controlled via laptop by a committee of 5 people
- **White lights** = no specific event

**Color Events:**
- **Blue** - Carolina Panthers touchdowns
- **Team colors** - ACC Championship games when teams score
- Various colors for community events, holidays, causes
- Wells Fargo operates "Wells Fargo Lights" program

**Architectural Design:**
- Exterior designed to resemble a **cut crystal**
- Distinctive "handlebar" roof design
- Green roof reduces stormwater runoff
