# Technology Stack: Charlotte NC Highway Vegetation

**Project:** Charlotte Digital Twin - Highway Roadside Vegetation
**Researched:** 2026-02-22

## Recommended Vegetation Stack

### Turf Grasses (Active Zone - Near Roadway)

| Species | Type | Seasonal Color | Texture | Coverage |
|---------|------|----------------|---------|----------|
| **Bermudagrass** (Cynodon dactylon) | Warm-season | Bright emerald (summer) / Tan-brown (winter dormant) | Medium-fine, dense | ~60% of maintained turf |
| **Tall Fescue** (Festuca arundinacea) | Cool-season | Dark green year-round | Coarse, wide blades | ~30% of maintained turf |
| **Zoysiagrass** (Zoysia spp.) | Warm-season | Yellow-green to olive (summer) / Golden-brown (winter) | Fine to medium, cushion-like | ~10% specialty areas |

**Rationale:** Charlotte sits in the transition zone where both warm and cool-season grasses grow. Bermudagrass dominates due to heat tolerance and rapid growth. Tall Fescue provides year-round green in shaded areas. NC State Extension confirms ~80% of Charlotte lawns use Tall Fescue, but highway applications favor Bermudagrass for durability.

### Highway Trees (Medians and Beyond Clear Zones)

#### Evergreen Trees

| Species | Height | Needle Characteristics | Common Use |
|---------|--------|------------------------|------------|
| **Loblolly Pine** (Pinus taeda) | 60-100+ ft | Yellow-green, 3 per bundle, 6-9" long | Most common roadside pine in Piedmont |
| **Shortleaf Pine** (Pinus echinata) | 50-80 ft | 2 per bundle, shorter than loblolly | Widespread, drought-tolerant |
| **Longleaf Pine** (Pinus palustris) | 60-120 ft | Dark green, 3 per bundle, 8-18" long | Coastal Plain, distinctive feathery look |
| **Eastern Red Cedar** (Juniperus virginiana) | 30-40 ft | Scale-like, dark green | Native evergreen, wildlife value |

#### Deciduous Trees

| Species | Height | Leaf Characteristics | Fall Color | Common Use |
|---------|--------|---------------------|------------|------------|
| **Willow Oak** (Quercus phellos) | 40-60 ft | Narrow, willow-like leaves | Yellow-brown | Fast-growing street/highway tree |
| **Northern Red Oak** (Quercus rubra) | 60-75 ft | Large, lobed leaves | Reddish-brown | Hardy, long-lived |
| **White Oak** (Quercus alba) | 60-100 ft | Rounded lobes, classic oak | Wine-red to brown | Majestic shade tree |
| **Sweetgum** (Liquidambar styraciflua) | 60-75 ft | Star-shaped leaves | Yellow/red/purple mix | Distinctive shape, adaptable |
| **Tulip Poplar** (Liriodendron tulipifera) | 70-90 ft | Unique tulip-shaped leaves | Clear yellow | Fast-growing, NC native |
| **Red Maple** (Acer rubrum) | 40-60 ft | 3-5 lobed leaves | Brilliant red | Most common eastern US tree |

**Rationale:** FHWA NC Native Plants list confirms all species above are recommended for roadside planting. Loblolly Pine dominates Charlotte highways. Oaks and Sweetgum provide canopy diversity.

#### Groundcover Junipers

| Species | Height | Foliage Color | Use Case |

### Urban Ornamental Trees (Interchanges, Exits)

| Species | Height | Features | Use Case |
|---------|--------|----------|----------|
| **Crape Myrtle** (Lagerstroemia indica) | 15-25 ft | Summer blooms 120 days, pollution tolerant | Highway interchanges, urban areas |
| **Flowering Dogwood** (Cornus florida) | 15-30 ft | NC state flower, white/pink bracts | Spring interest, native |
| **Eastern Redbud** (Cercis canadensis) | 20-30 ft | Pink-purple early spring flowers | Spring color, native |

**Rationale:** Crape Myrtles are extensively used in Charlotte urban landscaping due to long bloom period and pollution tolerance. Dogwood and Redbud are native alternatives.

## Seasonal Color Reference

### Bermudagrass Seasonal Cycle
| Season | Color | Texture | Notes |
|--------|-------|---------|-------|
| Spring (Apr-May) | Light green emerging | Medium | Green-up when soil reaches 65F |
| Summer (Jun-Aug) | Bright emerald green | Dense, uniform | Peak growing season |
| Fall (Sep-Oct) | Fading green to yellow | Thinning | Growth slows |
| Winter (Nov-Mar) | Tan to straw brown | Dormant | Completely brown, not dead |

### Tall Fescue Seasonal Cycle
| Season | Color | Texture | Notes |
|--------|-------|---------|-------|
| Spring | Dark green, vibrant | Coarse | Fastest growth period |
| Summer | Dark green (may fade without water) | Coarse | Susceptible to heat stress |
| Fall | Deep green | Coarse | Best growing season |
| Winter | Dark green (slower growth) | Coarse | Stays green year-round |

## Alternatives Considered

| Category | Recommended | Alternative | Why Not |
|----------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Turf | Bermudagrass | St. Augustinegrass | Less cold-hardy for Charlotte winters |
| Turf | Tall Fescue | Kentucky Bluegrass | Poor heat tolerance in NC summers |
| Native Grass | Switchgrass | Miscanthus | Miscanthus is invasive in some areas |
| Tree | Loblolly Pine | Virginia Pine | Slower growing, less adaptable |
| Shrub | Native roses | Knockout Rose | Knockout is cultivated, not native |
